Content

Home page

Brianna was drawn to baking out of curiosity and soon recognized that it provided an escape from her daily stress. As she taught herself to bake, her stress-relieving hobby quickly turned into a passion. She expanded her skills by practicing daily. She moved to Utah and began working in a restaurant. She worked throughout the day and returned home, only to bake or cook into the night. She enjoyed sharing her creations with others whenever possible. Close to a year later, she moved back to California. She wanted to perfect her recipes and recreate dishes inspired by her ancestors. She started working at a restaurant in Orange County, CA that served Mayan dishes. There, she learned what kind of work was required to create a new recipe. With the help of the other chefs, she researched recipes and tested new ways to create historic dishes. While working there, she met her now husband and they are happily expecting their first baby. Because the restaurant served Mayan food, Brianna left her job to focus on creating the Aztec dishes her family members once made. She is now focusing on her work at home and hopes to open her own restaurant soon.

About Brianna

At the age of three, Brianna started piano lessons. She played for five years and was a skilled pianist. As she grew, her interested shifted to marital arts. Her father and several other family members participated in Taekwondo with Brianna. She received her first-degree black belt in 20xx. Briannas interest in culinary arts and baking started in 2018. She taught herself to bake and discovered a new love. Her passion for creating foods and desserts led her to pursue a career in culinary arts. She continued to work on pastries and foods at home until 2021. She moved to Utah and began her first culinary job. Since then, Brianna has moved back to California and furthered her experience at other restaurants. She has married and is a soon-to-be mother. Her growing family drives her to continue to pursue her culinary dreams. Brianna plans to have a themed restaurant that offers a new twist to the food her ancestors made.
